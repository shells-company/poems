It's raining.\
The forecast lied.\
I expected sunshine and clear skies.\
I slipped, bruised my knee, I cried.

Limping now, I still push forward.\
The only escape, through the storm.\
So soaked that I lost the meaning of dry.

Sickness is approaching\
I'm shivering and cold.\
Yet I still refuse to fold.

My soul wishes for an end, yet

In extreme heat, I've wished for cold.\
In the summer, I've wished for the bloom of spring.\
In the spring, I've missed the holidays of winter.\
In the fall, I've wished for summer beach.\
and in the drought, I've wished for this very rain.

My sickness will heal, just as my knee.\
I will soon be dry and wish for\
this storm again.

Knowing it will pass I find the magic.\
To smile in this rain\
and enjoy the pain\
The rain is just rain after all.
